Our challenge is that there are limited spaces for innovators to explore their ideas and create viable prototypes that can be experimented on

> Background:Â What is the history of your challenge? What is causing or driving it? Who is involved? How does the current situation look like? What undesired effects does it produce?



Since the inception of the Acclab in Sierra Leone, we discovered that there are a lot of innovative thinking by young people and they are trying to create prototypes in their own spaces using mainly scrap materials collected from waste sites. These prototypes are usually a safety hazard due to the lack of proper fabrication materials and tools. Running experiments on them becomes a challenge. So we want to works with partners, mainly academic institutions, to establish fabrication labs in the four regions in Sierra Leone, so that young people with creative ideas can get the support they need closer to home to create prototypes and run tests safely.

We aim to ignite innovation for sustainable development by fostering a culture of design, innovation and resilience. Collaborating with academic institutions to leverage their technical knowledge and access to space to develop the skills of innovators and entrepreneurs, focusing on computer aided designs and use of modern equipment to fabricate prototypes. The regional fabrication labs will also be a space that facilitates exchange of ideas between young innovators and industry experts. Promoting a culture of innovation and incorporating it into educational curricula and community programs. The regional fab labs will also leverage on the work of the UniPod program, and ideas from the regions can feed into the UniPod for a more holistic approach.
