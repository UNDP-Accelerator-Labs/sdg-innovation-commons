Land Empowerment Animals People (LEAP) Spiral engage and empower communities in moving reactive conservation to proactive stewardship. Through their project, "Lower Kinabatangan Segama Wetlands Conservation: Advancing Indigenous Initiatives for Sustaining Fisheries, Mangroves, Forests and Wildlife in the Co-management of Malaysia’s Largest Ramsar Site", the solutions and activities carried out are as follow. Solutions: Open Data Kit system to determine income baseline and as a community-based fisheries monitoring program. They have received 700 datasets from over 400 fishermen since June 2020. The system allows each community member to extract data in Excel to see income levels, capital cost, and strategies for fisheries recovery plan.  Procurement of raw materials for community cleaning up activities. Created a database for orang utan and ongoing community mapping. Established community internet facilities- projectors, audio equipment, electricity control units and internet signal booster.
