> Background:Â What is the history of your challenge? What is causing or driving it? Who is involved? How does the current situation look like? What undesired effects does it produce?



Leveraging on the partnership established with the Copperbelt University (CBU) since 2020 as part of the National Innovation Initiative (NII), which identified 140 locally developed innovations with high potential for impact on the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), the TRAC 2 resources will support the transitioning from a UNDP co-led initiative to fully government-led one. The investment will support the graduate path of the Experimentation Programme of the initiative through the establishment of this UNIPOD within CBU. The UNIPOD will particularly focus on female students as UNDP has worked with various stakeholders to encourage young women and girls to embrace careers in Sciences, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM).

The National Innovation Initiative (NII) is under the leadership of the Ministry of Technology and Science and is implemented in close collaboration with the Zambia Information and Communications Technology Authority (ZICTA) the Patents and Companies Registration Agency (PACRA) and the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA). The proposed intervention will build upon previous collaborations between UNDP, through its Accelerator Lab, and the Copperbelt University (CBU) to promote the development of innovations through the National Innovation Initiative and the Experiment Programme to test innovations to generate evidence to inform their scale up.
