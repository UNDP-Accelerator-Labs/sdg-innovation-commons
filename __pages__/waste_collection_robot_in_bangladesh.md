The Bangladesh Accelerator Lab organized a Womenâ€™s Digital Innovation hackathon to promote ICT skills and innovation practices to female students and professionals and find new digital solutions to a wide range of SDG challenges. The winning solution that came up from this solution was one that aims to address the persistent challenge of waste detection, segregation, and collection. A Self-Controlling Spider Robot was designed by the first prize winning team of the hackathon. The robot's purpose is to collect, detect, and separate wastage for a model Smart City. The solution would use technologies such robot technology, sensor technology, artificial intelligence, machine learning, etc. The solution is particularly timely as garbage management is a huge challenge in Bangladesh and if not managed properly, not only will diseases increase but will also lead to soil, air and water pollution.
