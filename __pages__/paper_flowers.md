> Observation & Description: What is the innovation about? What need does it answer? What does it do?

This innovation is about the use of paper flowers from recycled materials.

> Need: What need or challenge does this answer?

Recycling used materials into new products that are attractive and saleable.

> User: Who is the solution for? How does it answer the need of the user? Why does it matter to the user?

For micro-entrepreneurs who would like to go into upcycling.

> Insight(s): What does this observation mean?  What have you learned from the observation?

Flowers have been used to beautify our churches, halls, and houses on specials occasions and even during burials. this has gone on for generations, and is not only done in our country.

This Flower vendor from Freedom in Carbon market had to adapt and find another way when the flower stocks started dwindling and the demands were expected to rise d/t upcoming events.

With a pinch of luck and a lot of hard work she came by some people selling paper flowers, a group of senior citizens. She partnered with them purchased their flowers and suggested to soak them in wax to make them shiny and waterproof, thus to prevent them from easily weathering in the sun and rain.

> Opportunities: What are other opportunities where the solution can be applied?

The use of renewable materials, these flowers were made with paper, and wax which was recycled from used candles.

This craft of creating paper flowers can be done with some elbow grease and a little practice and can be used to create livelihood programs.
