> Qualitative Results of the Innovation

1. Improved agricultural practices: Baobab organization has worked with local farmers to promote climate-smart agricultural practices, such as agroforestry, crop rotation, and conservation agriculture. These practices have helped farmers to improve their yields and adapt to changing weather patterns, thus increasing their resilience to climate change.
2. Increased access to clean water: Baobab organization has implemented projects aimed at improving access to clean water in border areas of Mali. These projects have included the construction of water wells and the distribution of water filters. By increasing access to clean water, Baobab organization has helped to reduce the impact of climate change on water resources in the region.
3. Strengthened community resilience: Baobab organization has worked with local communities to build their resilience to climate change through education and awareness-raising campaigns. These campaigns have helped to increase knowledge about the impacts of climate change and how to adapt to them, thus strengthening the community's ability to cope with climate-related shocks and stresses.

> Quantitative Result of the Innovation

1. Improved agricultural yields: Baobab organization's agroforestry project has resulted in an increase in crop yields of up to 50% in some areas.
2. Increased access to clean water: Baobab organization's water projects have provided access to clean water for over 20,000 people in border areas of Mali.
3. Strengthened community resilience: Baobab organization's education and awareness-raising campaigns have reached over 30,000 people in border areas of Mali, increasing their knowledge and understanding of climate change and its impacts.
