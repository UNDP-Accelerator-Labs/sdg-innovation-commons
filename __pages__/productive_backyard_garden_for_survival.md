> Observation & Description: What is the innovation about? What need does it answer? What does it do?

People at all levels are finding ways to survive during health crisis. This is a typical vacant area of a subdivision which was transformed into an urban garden using indigenous materials (readily available). 

> Need: What need or challenge does this answer?

Poverty and hunger particularly food access, availability, and affordability (security)

> User: Who is the solution for? How does it answer the need of the user? Why does it matter to the user?

For low-cost subdivisions and households at grassroots level. It will address food security. 

> Insight(s): What does this observation mean?  What have you learned from the observation?

Survival should start at home.

> Opportunities: What are other opportunities where the solution can be applied?

When scaled within the community, everyone becomes food secure and resilient.
