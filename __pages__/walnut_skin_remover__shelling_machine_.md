The walnut peeling machine is a Locally made equipment, assembled from local available and recycled materials. The walnut is poured directly into the metal barrel, and the walnuts are continuously rolled and rubbed in the barrel while rotating to remove the skin. Water is added manually to assist in making the skin and dirt on the surface fall off. The Machin is made of a metal bucket that is attached to a motor which works on not only electricity but also on battery to accommodate for the long hours powers cuts in coastal area There is a high demand and need for the machine especially during the walnut harvest season. The owner accepts nuts from small scale farmers in her village who want to peel their walnut harvest. How can UNDP support the innovator: UNDP can work on refining the model on the condition of replicating it to other farmers in different villages.
