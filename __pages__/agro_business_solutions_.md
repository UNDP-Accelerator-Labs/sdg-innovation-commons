> Qualitative Results of the Innovation

1. In 2020, Komaza planted over 1 million trees in Kenya, resulting in the sequestration of approximately 112,000 metric tons of carbon dioxide.
2. Komaza has provided over 27,000 farmers with training and support on sustainable forestry practices, resulting in the establishment of over 30,000 hectares of tree farms.
3. Komaza's microfinance program has disbursed over $3 million in loans to farmers since its inception, resulting in the establishment of over 9,000 hectares of tree farms and the creation of over 7,000 jobs.
 4. A 2019 study conducted by the World Agroforestry Centre found that the agroforestry model used by Komaza resulted in a 34% increase in maize yields and a 21% increase in income for farmers in the areas where it operates.
