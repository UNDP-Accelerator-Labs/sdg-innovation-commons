> Title of the innovation

Educating the youth in rural areas on the use of websites and computer skills

> Brief description of the innovation

The aim of this is to have young people in rural areas be trained in basic computer skills and internet skills that will enable them undertake jobs at national level and global level. Since there has been a negative impact that has driven some youths in rural areas to extreme levels of poverty, the skills that they will learn will enable them be part of the gig economy which might help them sustain a livelihood  if they have any at. 
Case in point is chisamba area in Zambia, most of the young people find difficulties to find jobs due to limited availability of jobs in the area. They live by finding any meagre job that comes once in a while. If they were introduced to computer skills they might be able to do virtual jobs that might enable them to start up businesses in farming and other sectors in the area.

> Commercial Viability of the Innovation

The training centre will help most youths in chisamba get computer skills that will enable them to do jobs that are computer related such as graphic design web design and other internet jobs. 
The target market are youths living in chisamba area that have gone up to junior and secondary education.
The pricing of the training programs will be affordable to many young people for they might go as low as k200 per course. The main aim is to empower young people with these computer skills that will allow them to be part of gig economy. 
The participants in these programs will be equipped to be part of the a bigger economy at national and International level. They will be able to use the world web to do business and improve the efficiency of their business operations.

> Technical Feasibility

The following is the implementation plan:
1. The sourcing of a building in chisamba that is big enough to accommodate some learners.
2. Source some infrastructure that will be categorised as follows:
A. 25 desktop computers 
B. Internet modems and suitable internet service provider. 
C. Desks for sitting and placing of computers of about 25 or more.
D. 2 whiteboards and also accessories such as whiteboard markers .
E.  Four instructors
F. 2 administration managers to manage the training centre.

> Anticipated Social Impact

The training programs will enable alot of youths to do virtual jobs found on the internet and also enable to efficiently use computerised systems employed by some farmers and other business people in the area.
