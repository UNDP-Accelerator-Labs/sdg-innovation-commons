> Challenge statement:Â What is your challenge? (Please answer in specific terms: "Our challenge is that...â€.)



Our challenge is that the potential of digital tools to de-risk financial investments in the environment and small enterprises and CSOs remains largely unrealized in Trinidad and Tobago despite a high level of digital and mobile penetration.

> Background:Â What is the history of your challenge? What is causing or driving it? Who is involved? How does the current situation look like? What undesired effects does it produce?



Since 2022, the government has taken serious interest in digitizing both economy and society and it has received financial support from various development partners, including the IADB, CAF and UNCDF. There is a significant thrust towards digital finance and many commercial banking institutions have launched mobile payment systems for smaller businesses. Despite this, certain key segments of the society remain woefully under included. Many of these are socially- and environmentally-oriented organisations who, in a more developed society, align to ESG principals and, it is hypothesised, would receive greater support. Besides the fact that the benefit of these activities is not fully understood in Trinidad and Tobago (as evidenced by interviews and workshops held over the last year with the EU delegation and prominent NGOs), there exists the view that their activities are somewhat risky compared to more traditional for-profit activities. Given the recent thrust towards digital finance and real-time data to de-risk investments, the UNDP Accelerator Lab is embarking on a challenge to improve the inclusion of such organisations over the course of the year focusing on the blue-green economy, and financial education to improve investment and invest-ability and the digitisation of social benefits platforms.Â
