> Description of the solution

Clean Air Now: A global network of CSOs working to improve air quality around the world. They provide resources, training materials, and support for community air monitoring programs

> Socio-economic effects

Increased public awareness about air quality issues
Empowered communities to hold authorities accountable for pollution control
Improved data collection for air quality management strategies
Potential for citizen science to inform policy decisions

> Other Notes

Community air monitoring programs can be a powerful tool for empowering citizens and promoting environmental justice. CSOs can play a crucial role in facilitating these programs, ensuring data quality, and advocating for policy action based on the collected data.
WE Move Europe: A European federation of environmental NGOs promoting clean air and sustainable mobility. They advocate for policies that support citizen science initiatives for air quality monitoring (https://action.wemove.eu/sign/2021-03-air-quality-petition-EN).
Greenpeace Southeast Asia: While their primary focus isn't solely on community air monitoring, Greenpeace is a prominent environmental CSO that has supported air quality monitoring initiatives in Southeast Asia (https://www.greenpeace.org/international/act/join-the-movement-for-clean-air/).
