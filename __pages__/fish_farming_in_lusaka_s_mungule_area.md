> Title of the innovation

Fish Farming in Lusaka's Mungule area

> Brief description of the innovation

Small scale fish farming working with the local community and using local materials. I employ people from the area to create employment whilst providing affordable nutritional food to the public.

> Do you have a prototype for your innovation?

- no

> Commercial Viability of the Innovation

The fish farming is employing people from the community providing wages to support their families hence they can educate themselves. The proceeds will be used to reinvest to grow the agric business to help employ more for enhanced community. The target market for the fish are the Lusaka supermarkets, institutions, restaurants and individual consumers.The challenge prize will help th business grow by financing sustainable solar power for water pumps, developing more fish ponds and refrigerated transport for preserveing fish yo the market.
The innovation will create economic value for the community and help improve their livelihood and eliminate hunger.

> Technical Feasibility

The business involves five open water ponds dug by local community and fed by bohole water. Fingerlings are bought from Lusaka with 3 weeks difference from pond to pond. A technically knowledgeable person supervises the business. Much of the feed would be produced mixing locally grown materials. The fish would be harvested in 3 weeks intervals. The market was surveyed and 5 supermarkets agreed to buy the fish with the excess to be supplied to major institutions and individuals. A hired refrigerated vehicle to be used to transport the fish. Some fish would be dried and smoked to supply the market. A team to manage the business include, the Farm Manager, an accountant, Specialist Fish Farming expert, driver and general workers.

> Anticipated Social Impact

The innovation will assist in reducing poverty in the local Mungule community by providing the currently scarce resources to buy food, educate young ones and improve nutrition.
Further the innovation will bring social economic activity in the community,  hence reduced alcohol abuse and improved health and well-being.
