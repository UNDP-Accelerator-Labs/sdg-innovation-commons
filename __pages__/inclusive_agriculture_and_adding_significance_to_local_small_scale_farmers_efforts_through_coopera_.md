> Title of the innovation

Inclusive Agriculture and adding significance to local small scale farmers efforts through cooperation and incorpation.

> Brief description of the innovation

Engaging in a wide range of agriculture practices and incorporating local all scale farmers  to claim significant market share.
Working with the little that is available 
and adding value.
Manage a piggery and free range village chicken on a business scale, fish farming and gardening also cultivating hot culture.

> Commercial Viability of the Innovation

Presently the national market of green farm products and locally produced white and red meat have deficits and prices have eccelerated due to low contribution on the locals to the marked, the international market is the larger contribute on our market.
The initiative will target to substantiate on what the local market is making available before in the long run take on the larg market share.
Through also cooperation with the local small scale farmers incorpation and finding a market for the put together commodities it will encourage hard work, social and individual sustainability among the locals in the area of operation.

> Technical Feasibility

The initiative is agricultural based with it's base on inclusive agricultural practice that is non hazardous to the soil and green climate friendly.
Taking advantage of our geographical location with vast land and sarounded with many water bodies. Agriculture has been seen as a way of responding to the challenges faced on lowly income non industrialized areas of our country in line with the National development plan.
Through inclusive agriculture (piggery, goat and village chickens) will be channelling waste products to uses as organic fertilizer in production of fresh farm products and fish feeding  and using safe non land degradation agricultural practices.

> Anticipated Social Impact

Development self reliance among youths by working with what they have were they are with what they have.
The initiative will supplement in supplementing unemployment and deficits in locally produced high quality non GMO farm produce.
