## Data and Methods

> Relating to your types of data, why did you chose these? What gaps in available data were these addressing?

The Lab generated a novel dataset about the core infrastructure of Zanzibar city, mapping many features such as roads, waterways, buildings and trash sites. The data has revealed the existence of new buildings that had previously not been registered by government officials. This information is being used by the cityâ€™s Urban Water Supply and Sanitation Authority to add waste collection points to serve the previously unmapped informal settlements.

> Why was it necessary to apply the above innovation method on your frontier challenge? How did these help you to unpack the system?

It took the team less than a month to generate a huge amount of novel data, labeled and validated, using the virtual mapathon method and an existing platform for work allocation. Mapathons, or other short-term 'blitz' methods, are frequently employed in participatory science to assist cover data shortages. If your collective intelligence initiative does not have or require dedicated volunteers, this strategy may be a good fit.
