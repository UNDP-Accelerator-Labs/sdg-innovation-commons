Pn Aizah started MADcash when COVID-19 hit Malaysia. MADCash, which stands for Multiply, Assist and Donate Cash is a platform that allows donors to channel their money to B40 women entrepreneurs launching new businesses. The donated funds are used as loans of between RM1,000 to RM2,000 which is treated as a perpetual cash fund. MADCash also raises a separate fund to run an entrepreneurship development programme that equips these women to strengthen their businesses. Through the MADCash programme, women receive the benefits of mentorship and training by the Academy of Women Entrepreneurs to learn the ins and outs of running a business.   “Instead of giving your money to charity, donate it to MADCash, impact lives and track who your funds are helping at any one time.” Pn Aizah pitches this idea to banks, foundations, NGOs and the CSR arm of corporate organisations for them to help women become financially and digitally included. As the women entrepreneurs pay back their nointerest loans within 10 months, this money is upcycled to another woman. Pn Aizah stresses that MADCash is not a bank and does not operate like one. MADCash is straightforward, which gives her the edge compared to other micro-loans programmes. MADCash follows the Islamic Social Finance Model.   Another aspect of the MADCash programme that sets it apart is that it is a holistic support system. The women in the programme may also experience difficulties in personal areas.
