> Title of the innovation

AGRICULTURE - HIGH VALUE GROPS

> Brief description of the innovation

Growing high value crops like sweet corn, green beans, green peas, cowpeas, sweet potatoes, Irish potatoes, water melons, cucumbers, soya beans, sunflower, sugar beans and groundnuts

> Do you have a prototype for your innovation?

- no

> Commercial Viability of the Innovation

The innovation will generate income by continuously growing the crops on demand and by expanding their farms as the need to grow more to meet the demand and make a profit at the end of the day
Which in turn will improve their families and the community they live in by even creating employments for youth and women
Our targeted markets are near by towns livingstone, choma, mazabuka, and Lusaka and sorounded communities
Our offtakes would be supermarkets like spar, shop rite, pick and pay general hospitals, and green markets.
Profits of these businesses will help to expand and grow their business sustainably
The value additions to these business will be from soya beans and sunflower they will be able to make cookies, cooking oil, soya cakes and make animal feeds which in turn will create more income

> Technical Feasibility

Our target is 500 farmers which are 500 families of 6 from 5 different farming camps namely nalube, nakeempa, simukanka, gamers and siamuba camps
Eah camp will have a coordinator and 5 trained field officers to monitor and supervise the farmers manage their farms well in terms of soil treatment and crop lotation
Distribution of inputs will be done by camp coordinators depending on the size of the farm
Inputs will be fertilisers, vegetables seeds, and legumes seeds, chemicals and maize

> Anticipated Social Impact

The innovation will improve the beneficialies by having stable and continuous income which in turn will sustain their farming business and it will help change the behaviour of farmers to understand the importance of turning farming into commercial business that will generate income through out the year as compared to seasonal farming
