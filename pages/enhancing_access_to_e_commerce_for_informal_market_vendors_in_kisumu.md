## Design
**Explain the design of the experimental activity. In general, experimental activities consist on trying to learn how results are connected to a stimuli.**

> What is the specific learning intent of the activity?

To develop an e-commerce platform using HCD and onboard 300 female informal market vendors in Kisumu to enhance access to the digital economy as a lever for enhanced business resilience and strengthening livelihoods

> What is your hypothesis? IF... THEN....

If female entrepreneurs in informal markets in Kisumu can access e-commerce platforms then they can increase their daily incomes and overall positively impact the socio-economic facets of their lives.

> Does the activity use a control group for comparison?

- Yes, the same group but before the intervention

> How is the intervention assigned to different groups in your experiment?

- Random assignment

> Describe which actions will you take to test your hypothesis:

1) Refine the experiment plan in collaboration with the partner 2) Collect baseline data on the female entrepreneurs in the various informal markets in Kisumu 3) Analyse the data to determine key characteristics and possible behavioural nudges for the experiment 4) Use HCD to design an e-commerce platform (MVP) 5) Onboard and train 300 female traders on the platform  6) Monitor and track the data from the platform for the duration of the experiment 7) Collect end-project data from the users and develop insights 8) Disseminate the findings and explore opportunities for connecting with relevant partners to scale the learnings

> What is the unit of analysis of this experimental activity?

300 female market vendors in Kisumu

> Please describe the data collection technique proposed

Use of interviews, focus group discussions and ethnographic visits to get qualitative and quantitative data from the female vendors Financial transaction data and customer reaction from the e-commerce platform

> What is the timeline of the experimental activity? (Months/Days)

6 months
