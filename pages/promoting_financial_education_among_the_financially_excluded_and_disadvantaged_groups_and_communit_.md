> Title of the innovation

Promoting financial education among the financially excluded and disadvantaged groups and communities in Zambia.

> Brief description of the innovation

The organization is engaged in promoting financial education to all age groups in Zambia especially those who are financially excluded. These categories involve inmates, social cash transfer beneficiaries ,marketeers small scale farmers, children, youths adults who are disadvantaged in one way or the other. The owner managers of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprise ( MSMEs) are required to acquire knowledge, skills, confidence, good financial behavior and conduct to make informed decisions that is beneficial to themselves,  family, society, businesses and the country as whole. Financial literacy has attracted attention around the world because of its importance in financial decision making. A number of economic, demographic and social developments have further highlighted its significance. These include over-indebtedness, financial scams, financial crises, globalization, changing demographics and sophisticated and complex financial products and services. Therefore the innovation is aiming to reduce poverty levels and inequalities ( SDG 1), increase public safety, ensure second chances for inmates and reduce higher recidivism/repeat offenders, promote financial stability that will serve as a stimulus to economic growth among all citizens.

> Commercial Viability of the Innovation

Our company is Not for profit entity. However as a Social enterprise we shall generate income through research and consultancy. We have collaborated with Sunbridge Education Centre in Livingstone to offer quality education to our community. We are also making partnerships with other corporate entities to meet SDG 17 for sustainability purpose. Financial education is an important tool to assist in improving operations of existing businesses like Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises ( MSME) to achieve financial capability and improve their investment portfolio. Individuals too benefit from acquiring financial knowledge that change their financial wellness and quality of decisions to invest and grow.
The Challenge prize will help the foundation grow in several ways. More financial education clubs would be created at Prisons and Markets to offer trainings to the targeted groups. Further more Business desks will be set-up at more than 30 Correctional facilities to absorbs inmates and ex-mates who wish to form businesses or join cooperatives after re-entry into society. The Prize money would also bring more e-business and efficiency through ICT configuration to produce timely financial reports, research  work, analysis and what if scenario data output.
The foundation is very much concerned with criminal pollution in terms of financial scums and cyber crimes imposed on society and environment. Through social and financial education, inmates would have  confidence to run businesses within the law and achieve  free streets, environment and society with  all SDGs met.

> Technical Feasibility

The innovation is designed around the National Strategy on Financial Education ( NSFE II) for Zambia. The Republic of Zambia have among other goals to achieve a financially educated population by 2030. The National Financial Inclusion Strategy (NFIS) indicates that there is a need to improve financial literacy levels in Zambia. This status is also reported by World Bank Report of 2017 dubbed 'Enhancing Financial Capability'. The financial education innovation and programs are centered around the four themes as follows:
   i)  Financial education for children
   ii) Financial education for youths
   iii) Financial education for adults
   iv) Financial education for all age groups.
Our organization is involved in the financial education for all age groups in Zambia. Depending on several factors, including the characteristics of the target audience(s) financial literacy topics will involve:
    a) Living within ones' means
    b) Goal setting and budgets                         i) identifying frauds
    c) Saving and Banking                                   j) Taxation
    d) Insurance                                                  k)  Entrepreneurship
    e) Investments and stocks
    f) Retirement and Pension
    g) Financial products &amp; services
    h) Consumer protection

> Anticipated Social Impact

Financial literacy is a major determinant of demand for financial services. Financial illiteracy is widespread among the youths, elderly ,women, the poor, less educated and the underprivileged. Since financial literacy is a combination of awareness, knowledge, skill, attitude and behavior necessary to make sound financial decision and ultimately achieve individual financial wellbeing. The world as a whole and Zambia in particular has been affected by causes of over-indebtedness, financial scam, financial crises ,globalization and changing demographics. Therefore anticipated social impact derived from consumer insight is immeasurable. Households, MSMEs,  SMEs, Communities, governments and the economy would benefit greatly if such innovation is implemented and supported. The proposed innovation is relevant to the current market because nations, corporate governance institutions, individuals, targeted groups, free streets and environment gain more from financially educated population, is likely to save and invest more, to be better equipped to run successful businesses and use financial products that are appropriate to their circumstances. This would help reduce levels of financial exclusion and poverty.
