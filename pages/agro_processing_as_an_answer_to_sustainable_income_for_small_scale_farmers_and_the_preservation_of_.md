> Title of the innovation

Agro processing as an answer to sustainable income for small scale farmers and the preservation of the environment. This project is aimed at providing market for the farmers yield by removing middlemen and educating farmers on sustainable agriculture methods.

> Brief description of the innovation

The innovation aims at alleviation of poverty in Mungwi District of Northern province. The prototype of our innovation include a total change of mindset  by the farmer. This will include educating farmers on better farming methods, farming as a business and providing ready markets for the farmers' produced. The innovation aims at improving nutrition by eradicating starch dominated diets. We also aim at introducing a commercially viable crop which will generate sustainable income for the farmer. We have identified soybeans as a crop that farmers should focus on, the soybeans will be bought by our company which will be engaged in edible oil production. So the farmer will have a ready market for their produce
 The farmers in mungwi depend so much on charcoal  burning for their livelihood but bring such a facility near to them will encourage them to grow soybeans which will give them more money than charcoal. Future plans. Agro processing of soybeans in Mungwi will crate employment for the local community and completely eradicate poverty in the area. The innovation will include making charcoal briquettes from maize cobs at a later stage.

> Commercial Viability of the Innovation

The innovation will sale cooking oil which is the end product of soya bean production. There is no cooking oil manufacturing plant in Northern province and the cooking oil consumed come from lusaka which is more than 800 kilometers away.
Future plans include animal feed production from the soya cake which is a by product of cooking oil production.
The value addition to the environment is that charcoal burning will be reduced drastically as farmers will have a sustainable way of fending for their families.

> Technical Feasibility

We undertook a feasibility study on our farm in Mungwi to assess the viability of soybeans growth in the area and we have Lso identified a ready market for edible oil in Northern, luapula, muchinga, central and Eastern provinces. The agro processing innovation entails establishing a processing plant in Northern province which will consume the soybeans by processing it into edible oil. The company will sign MOU's with cooperatives in the area for Purchase of soya beans. The company will educate the farmer with the help of agriculture extention officers on better farm managemwnt (better methods of farming, financial management, farming as a business etc)

> Anticipated Social Impact

The innovation will change the lives of farmers in the area including women and youths. During our feasibility study we engaged farmers to work on our farm as we grew soyabeans, the community was delighted to make some extra income to take their children to school as well as buy necessities for their homes. The innovation will have a ripple effect as levels of seduction will increase and nutrition will also improve
