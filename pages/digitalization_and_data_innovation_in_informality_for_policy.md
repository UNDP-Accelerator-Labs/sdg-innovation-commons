## Design
**Explain the design of the experimental activity. In general, experimental activities consist on trying to learn how results are connected to a stimuli.**

> What is the specific learning intent of the activity?

The specific learning intents of the activity are to know: 1. What is the understanding among different stakeholders about the situation of informality in Bangladesh? 2. How informal workers can be benefitted from digitalization of informal business? 3. How a scale-up program would gradually benefit the existing system by adjusting the digital data tools and innovation based on learning experiences in the informal economy?

> What is your hypothesis? IF... THEN....

H1: IF digitalization and data intelligence can be scaled up, THEN the informal workers will be financially benefited through using digital financial tools; H2: IF good practices of digitalization and data innovation can be mapped and implemented, THEN it will create more income opportunities as well as increase the income of the informal workers; H3: IF data innovation on informality can be utilised based on data intelligence, THEN it will attract the attention of policy makers to contribute to the vulnerabilities of informal sectors and employment; H4: IF Digitalization and the use of data innovation and data intelligence can be properly managed, THEN it will meet the needs of the sectoral employment-including digital sensemaking, online job creation, online service delivery etc.

> Does the activity use a control group for comparison?

- Yes, a different group entirely
- Yes, the same group but before the intervention

> How is the intervention assigned to different groups in your experiment?

- Random assignment

> Describe which actions will you take to test your hypothesis:

Run full package of experimentation, data collection, monitoring and Evaluation of the indicators and conduction of research.

> What is the unit of analysis of this experimental activity?

1. Collective group of informal business owners and informal workers at different location in Bangladesh; 2. Recommendations on actions and processes from stakeholders; 3. Different module and products of data innovation in Informality for policy.

> Please describe the data collection technique proposed

Simple random sampling for pilot survey using KoboToolbox by physical or online one-to-one interview. For nation-wide survey, cluster random sampling might be applied.
