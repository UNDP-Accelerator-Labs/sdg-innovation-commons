## Learnings on your challenge

> What are the top 5 key insights you generated about your frontier challenge during this Action Learning Plan?

Villagers possess a rich understanding of the issue at hand. They possess the local wisdom to tackle problems, but government processes lack the flexibility to effectively utilize this approach. Despite acknowledging the issue, rigid structures and laws limit the state's ability to adapt. Through the villagers' attempts to create prototypes, they have realized they have the ability to take initiative without relying solely on government support. This shift in perspective transforms them from passive recipients to active contributors. It should be noted that the problem area is located in mountainous terrain, making it distinct from flat areas and requiring unique solutions that cannot simply be imported from elsewhere.

## Data and Methods

> Relating to your types of data, why did you chose these? What gaps in available data were these addressing?

Community perspectives and opinions are valuable and frequently overlooked in development processes. Despite being most affected by air pollution, they are often ignored. Direct engagement with the community allowed us to consider unique issues and perspectives not otherwise considered by the state. The state's top-down focus on law enforcement can be inflexible and does not always take into account local context."
