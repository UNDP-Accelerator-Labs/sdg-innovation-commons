It is the mechanism used for the economic retribution of the actions that indigenous communities carry out for the conservation of the upper part of the sub-basin of the Ixtacapa River. The actions have a positive impact on the maintenance of water resources in the sub-basin.

IThe main actors are nine K'iche' communities in the municipality of Nahualá, department of Sololá, with a total of 4500 people as users of the service, which also benefits five municipalities (90,000 inhabitants) and six private farms that use river water for agriculture and livestock, as well as a sugar mill.
