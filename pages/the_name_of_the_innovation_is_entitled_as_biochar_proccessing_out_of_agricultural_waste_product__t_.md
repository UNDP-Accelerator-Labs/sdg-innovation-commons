> Brief description of the innovation

Fisrt and fore most I shall briefly decribe what bichar is all about.
Biochar is a carbon rich material obtained from thermochemical conversion (slow, intermediate, and fast pyrolysis) of biomass in an oxygen-limited environment. It can be produced from a range of feedstock, including forest and agriculture residues, such as straw, nut shells, rice hulls, wood chips/pellets, tree bark, and switch grass.The importance of these wastes for the production of biochar is an efficient way to turn waste to useful and value-added substance
Biochar has been described as a possible tool for soil fertility improvement, potential toxic element adsorption, and climate change mitigation. Indeed, several studies have shown that biochar application to soil can improve soil physical and chemical properties, enhance plant nutrient availability and correlated growth and yield, increase microbial population and activities and reduce greenhouse gas emissions through Carbon sequestrion. Its impact as soil amendment enhances soil quality and plant growth with increased crop yield. Although, biochar resource, production process, soil type and condition as well as the type of crop to be planted could influence its effectiveness. Production of biochar ranges from the small scale using a cooking stove to large scale using pyrolysis system.
