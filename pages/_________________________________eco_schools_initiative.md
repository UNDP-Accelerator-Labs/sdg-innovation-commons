> Description of the solution

The Eco-Schools Initiative is an international program that empowers students to become active participants in creating sustainable schools and communities. It encourages schools to integrate environmental education into the curriculum and implement environmentally friendly practices throughout their operations.

The program focuses on seven key environmental themes: Water, Energy, Waste, Biodiversity, Health and Well-being, Climate Change, Food

Schools participating in the Eco-Schools Initiative follow a seven-step process that guides them through planning, taking action, monitoring progress, and achieving accreditation.

> Socio-economic effects

Raises awareness of environmental issues among students and communities

Promotes sustainability practices in schools and beyond

Empowers students to take action for a healthier planet

Improved air quality (potential indirect effect through reduced energy use, waste management, and green landscaping)

> Other Notes

Affected sectors are: Education, Environment, Air Quality (potential indirect effect).

The Eco-Schools Initiative provides a framework and resources to help schools implement environmental sustainability practices. It can be adapted to fit the specific context of each school. The Arabic resources highlight the program's application in Morocco and the bilingual guide from WWF offers a comprehensive overview. The program's focus on energy conservation, waste reduction, and green landscaping can contribute to improved air quality, although this might be an indirect effect.
