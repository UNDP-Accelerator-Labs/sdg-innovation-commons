> Title of the innovation

EmployMe Foundation

> Brief description of the innovation

A twofold model solution to youth unemployment crisis in Zambia.

> Do you have a prototype for your innovation?

- no

> Commercial Viability of the Innovation

EmployMe Foundation follows a twofold model in attaining itâ€™s goal:
 Firstly, it seeks to link struggling unemployed youths to employment and paid internship opportunities.
Secondly, it seeks to fund struggling unemployed youths living in poverty with startup funds to set up their own businesses or upscale their already existing businesses. The main aim of this initiate is to subsequently make youths employers in the near future. This initiative uses the principle of revolving funds; that is, a number of youths with feasible businesses apply for funding and later repay with low interest. The profits are then reinvested and given to another set of youths for the purpose of upscaling the program.

> Technical Feasibility

A twofold model designed to eradicate youth unemployment and poverty by linking  educated and uneducated youths to employment, paid internships  and funding to start up their own businesses and become self reliant though the principal of revolving funds.

> Anticipated Social Impact

This innovation will improve the lives of Zambian youths by providing a solution to the unemployment crisis by catering for both educated and uneducated youths at the same time encouraging entrepreneurship and innovation mindsets so as to subsequently make youths employers in the near future.          
This initiative also addresses underlying negative issues youths in Zambia are engaging in due to unemployment such as drug abuse, early teenage pregnancy and crime, it does so by empowering and helping youths find their place and contribute to a more prosperous and stable Zambia.
