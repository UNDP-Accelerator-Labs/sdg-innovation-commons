> Observation & Description: What is the innovation about? What need does it answer? What does it do?

Future-proofing the products, services and ideas that will be developed by the Institutional Management Enterprise (IME) Program through heritage-based creative entrepreneurship for, with and by the stakeholders of the institution - administrators, faculty, personnel, students, parents, alumni and community extension partners (indigenous groups and emerging urban cottage industry-based communities). The program is facilitated by the 5 units of the Center for Innovation and Social Ventures in coordination with the Community Extension Office. 

> Need: What need or challenge does this answer?

Capacity- and capability-building for sustainability of stakeholders. 

> User: Who is the solution for? How does it answer the need of the user? Why does it matter to the user?

> Insight(s): What does this observation mean?  What have you learned from the observation?

Revisiting the current initiatives and strategies of CISV allowed me to reflect on its focus and integration.  

> Opportunities: What are other opportunities where the solution can be applied?

Aligning curriculum and instruction to faculty and student development, research and community extension provides a best practice or communities of practice templates for academic institutions to conceptualize the circularity of their programs to seamlessly engage with the government, industry, academe, media and society.
